run_ncl_share_sensitivity.m — NCL Share Sensitivity
====================================================

WHAT IT DOES
------------
Studies how the amount of flexible (non-critical) load
available at each bus affects ES performance.

Sweeps: NCL share = [20%, 30%, 40%, 50%]
Fixed:  N_ES_max = 10, u_min = 0.20, rho_max = 0.80

INTERPRETATION
--------------
Higher NCL share = more load can be adjusted = ES is more effective.
Expected: fewer ES units needed as NCL share increases.

PRACTICAL MEANING
-----------------
NCL share represents what fraction of load is "flexible" —
appliances and processes that can be curtailed without disruption
(water heaters, pool pumps, HVAC in flexible mode, etc.).

If only 20% of load is flexible, ES needs to be deployed at
many more buses to achieve the same voltage improvement as
when 50% of load is flexible.
