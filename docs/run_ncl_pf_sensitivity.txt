run_ncl_pf_sensitivity.m — NCL Power Factor Sensitivity
========================================================

WHAT IT DOES
------------
Studies whether resistive or inductive NCL loads change ES
effectiveness in voltage support.

This connects to the physical ES literature where:
  - Resistive NCL (heaters, resistors): ES works on P component
  - Inductive NCL (motors, coils): ES works on both P and Q components

At OPF level, this difference is captured by Q_NCL:
  Resistive (PF=1.00):  Q_NCL = 0 (no reactive contribution)
  Inductive (PF=0.95):  Q_NCL = P_NCL * tan(acos(0.95)) = 0.33 * P_NCL
  Inductive (PF=0.90):  Q_NCL = P_NCL * tan(acos(0.90)) = 0.48 * P_NCL
  Inductive (PF=0.85):  Q_NCL = P_NCL * tan(acos(0.85)) = 0.62 * P_NCL

EXPECTED RESULT
---------------
For lagging (inductive) NCL, reducing u(j,t) also reduces Q_NCL.
Since voltage drop depends on both R*P + X*Q, reducing reactive
load additionally helps voltage — so ES may be MORE effective
with inductive NCL than resistive NCL.

However, the total apparent power S_NCL is also larger for
inductive NCL, so ES capacity (rho) rating must account for this.

HOW TO INTERPRET
----------------
If PF=0.85 gives better Vmin than PF=1.00 with same N_ES_max:
  Inductive NCL provides better voltage support capability.
If SumRho is larger for PF=0.85:
  ES needs more capacity to handle the reactive component.
