MODULE 4 — 24-Hour SOCP OPF with Reactive Support (Qg)
========================================================
File: module4.m (optimization.m)

WHAT IT DOES
------------
Runs a 24-hour SOCP (Second-Order Cone Program) OPF on the IEEE 33-bus
network WITH reactive power injection (Qg) at selected buses.

Objective: minimize total feeder losses weighted by time-of-use price
  min  sum_t [ price(t) * Loss(t) + lambda * sum_j |Qg(j,t)| ]

The Qg variables represent reactive support (e.g., capacitor banks,
inverters) — NOT Electric Spring. This is the intermediate step
between pure DistFlow and the ES model.

WHY RUN IT
----------
Baseline with conventional reactive support. Compare against Module 7
(ES-based OPF) to quantify the incremental benefit of ES over Qg.

OUTPUT
------
Saves voltage profiles and loss data to ./out_socp_opf_gurobi/.

REQUIRES
--------
YALMIP toolbox + Gurobi solver

DEPENDS ON
